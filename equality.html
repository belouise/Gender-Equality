ABOUT

Gender equality is about ensuring that all individuals, regardless of their gender identity or expression, have equal rights, opportunities, and treatment in society. It advocates for the belief that everyone should have the same access to resources, responsibilities, and opportunities regardless of their gender.

At its core, gender equality challenges traditional norms, stereotypes, and discrimination based on gender. It seeks to dismantle barriers that limit individuals' potential and reinforce inequalities. Gender equality is not about advocating for one gender over another but rather recognizing that all genders should have equal rights and opportunities.

Equal Rights

Ensuring that individuals of all genders have equal legal rights and protections under the law. This includes rights such as the right to vote, own property, access education and healthcare, and participate in decision-making processes.

Equal Opportunities

Providing equal access to opportunities in areas such as education, employment, leadership positions, and personal development. Gender equality aims to eliminate discrimination and biases that may prevent individuals from accessing these opportunities based on their gender.

Ending Gender-Based Violence

Working to eradicate violence and discrimination that specifically targets individuals based on their gender. This includes addressing issues such as domestic violence, sexual harassment, rape, and harmful traditional practices.

Empowering All Genders

Empowering individuals of all genders to fulfill their potential and pursue their aspirations without being limited by societal expectations or discriminatory practices.

UNITED NATIONS WOMEN

Ambassador

Emma Watson served as a prominent figure in UN Women's efforts to promote gender equality and women's empowerment. In 2014, Emma Watson was appointed as a UN Women Goodwill Ambassador, with a focus on advocating for the HeForShe campaign. As a Goodwill Ambassador, she used her platform and influence to raise awareness about gender inequality and mobilize support for the campaign's goals.

Emma Watson's involvement with UN Women contributed to raising awareness and mobilizing support for gender equality on a global scale. Her advocacy efforts helped to amplify UN Women's message and inspire individuals of all genders to take action towards building a more inclusive and equitable world.

HISTORY

19th Century

The Seneca Falls Convention in 1848 and the suffrage campaigns led by Suffragettes in the United Kingdom and the United States.

20th Century

This era saw significant activism and advocacy for reproductive rights, workplace equality, and an end to gender-based discrimination and violence.

21st Century

Today's era emphasizes intersectionality, recognizing that gender inequality intersects with other forms of discrimination based on factors such as race, class, sexual orientation, and disability
